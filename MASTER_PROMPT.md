## How to Use the ARIA System

### Step 1: Activate
Copy the prompt block above into your Claude conversation as the system prompt, or paste it as the first message in a new conversation.

### Step 2: State Your Research Context
Provide your research area, rough idea, or existing work. Be as specific as possible about:
- Your domain and sub-field
- What you already know / have done
- Your constraints (time, resources, data availability)
- Your target venue (if known)

### Step 3: Activate Skills Sequentially
Use the activation commands to engage specific skill frameworks:

- **Starting from scratch**: `ACTIVATE: IDEATION`
- **Mapping the literature**: `ACTIVATE: LITERATURE`
- **Designing methodology**: `ACTIVATE: METHODOLOGY`
- **Analyzing data**: `ACTIVATE: DATA`
- **Writing sections**: `ACTIVATE: WRITING`
- **Pre-submission review**: `ACTIVATE: QUALITY`
- **Journal targeting**: `ACTIVATE: JOURNAL`
- **Ethics check**: `ACTIVATE: ETHICS`
- **Citation strategy**: `ACTIVATE: CITATION`
- **Post-publication**: `ACTIVATE: IMPACT`
- **End-to-end workflow**: `ACTIVATE: FULL_WORKFLOW`
- **Fast-track protocol**: `ACTIVATE: RAPID_PUBLISH`

### Step 4: Iterate
The ARIA system is designed for iterative refinement. After each skill activation, review the output, provide feedback, and move to the next skill or re-engage the same one for deeper analysis.

### Cognitive Architecture Discipline

The five-step cognitive architecture (Summit Scan → Gap Analysis → Non-Obvious Bridge → Adversarial Stress Test → Synthesis) runs silently before every response. This ensures that every output is calibrated to the highest possible standard, not just the first acceptable answer.
