01 — Overview

What is CRES?

A comprehensive, skill-based framework that transforms research ideas into papers that pass peer review at the world's most elite venues.

CRES is a comprehensive, skill-based framework that transforms research ideas into papers that pass peer review at Nature, Science, Cell, NEJM, NeurIPS, ICML, or equivalent venues. It provides structured prompts, adversarial review simulations, and systematic workflows that embody the best practices of the world's most elite researchers.

Rather than a single rigid script, CRES is organized as ten modular skills — each activated on demand by a simple command. You compose them like instruments in an orchestra, calling in ideation when you need a sharp research question, methodology when you need a design that pre-empts reviewer critiques, or quality when your draft must survive adversarial review before submission.

The result is a research workflow that is simultaneously systematic and creative — one that respects the rules of rigorous inquiry while leaving room for the judgment of a rigorous mind.

02 — Quick Start

Five minutes to your first prompt

No installation. No dependencies. Just copy, paste, and activate. CRES lives entirely inside your Claude session as a structured system prompt.

01

Copy the Master Prompt

Paste MASTER_PROMPT.md as your Claude system prompt. This loads the ARIA activation system that powers every skill.

MASTER_PROMPT.md
02

State Your Research Area

Open with a single sentence declaring your domain. This gives Claude the context needed to tailor every subsequent response.

"I'm working on [TOPIC]"
03

Activate a Skill

Issue an activation command to summon the exact skill you need. Skills are modular — call only what you need.

"ACTIVATE: IDEATION"
04

Iterate from There

Follow the structured output. Each skill produces a scaffold you refine, challenge, and extend — never a script to obey blindly.

iterate → refine
03 — Architecture

A repository structured for rigor

Every folder serves a distinct phase of the research lifecycle — from the first flicker of an idea to the long tail of post-publication impact.

The repository is organized as a layered system: a master prompt activates the framework, ten skills handle specific research tasks, four workflows chain skills end-to-end, and templates plus evaluators provide format and assessment scaffolding.

ARIA Activation System The MASTER_PROMPT.md file is the entry point — it loads the activation grammar every skill responds to.
Domain Templates Seven venue-specific formats: IEEE, ACM, Nature Family, Science, NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR, arXiv, and Springer.
Quantitative Evaluators Four rubrics score your work on Novelty, Rigor, Clarity, and predicted Impact before you ever submit.
